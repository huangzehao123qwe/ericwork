# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Design Directors, Hiring Managers, and senior ICs at Staff/Principal-level companies (Intuit, Microsoft, Wealthsimple, Stripe, Databricks). Viewing on desktop, typically during a candidate screen or follow-up after a cold outreach. Busy, skeptical of claims, evaluating fit in under 2 minutes. They want to know: can this person think at scope, ship with craft, and operate as a peer?

Secondary: peer designers doing mutual portfolio research.

## Product Purpose

Eric Huang's professional portfolio. Communicates 10 years of product design experience, 8 in enterprise software, with depth in agentic UX, compliance tooling, and admin-heavy surfaces. The portfolio's job is to earn an interview by demonstrating systems thinking, craft, and critical thinking — not by describing it.

Success: a hiring manager finishes a case study and forwards the link.

## Brand Personality

Technical, minimal, honest. Work speaks first. No preamble, no self-congratulation. Confidence expressed through restraint, not volume.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages: purple gradients, hero metric cards, identical card grids, big numbers with small labels. The impeccable "hero-metric template" ban applies here.
- Dribbble-style portfolios: all visual polish, no evidence of thinking.
- Flashy agency portfolios: heavy scroll animations, overstyled transitions, design as performance.

Reference: davidlng.com — sparse, name-first, work speaks. That register of quiet confidence.

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't tell.** No bullet that starts "I am a strategic designer." Show the decision, show the outcome, let the reader conclude.
2. **Work earns the space.** Every section exists because it adds to the case — not because portfolios are supposed to have it. Cut ruthlessly.
3. **Expert confidence through restraint.** Staff-level communication is precise and unhurried. No enthusiasm punctuation. No filler transitions.
4. **Hierarchy does the work.** A recruiter skimming for 30 seconds should land on the same three things as a hiring manager reading for 5 minutes.
5. **Honest about scope.** Don't overstate. Don't understate. Show the edges of the work too — what was cut, what was learned.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. No motion required for navigation. Sufficient contrast for text on both light and image-heavy backgrounds. Case study images must not be the only carrier of meaning.
