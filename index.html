Design Philosophy

How I think about design

After 8+ years in enterprise UX, three principles consistently shape how I approach complex problems — from AI agents to mobile warehouse workflows.

Principle 01

Context is the experience.

In enterprise systems, the way you configure something is just as important as what it does. The mental model matters as much as the interface. When I design configuration surfaces, I treat context-setting as a first-class UX moment — not an afterthought.

Principle 02

Trust is earned in layers.

Whether it's an AI agent or a procurement workflow, users build confidence through small, consistent wins — not grand promises. I design for the skeptic first: if the most cautious user trusts it, everyone else will too.

Principle 03

Constraints are the brief.

Complex enterprise requirements, tight mobile screens, and competing stakeholders aren't obstacles — they're the forcing function for sharper decisions. The best design work I've done has come from leaning into constraints, not fighting them.
